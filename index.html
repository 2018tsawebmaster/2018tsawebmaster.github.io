Technology and Engineering Classes

    AP Computer Science Principles

    APCSP is an introductory course to computers and programming. This course focuses heavily on the workings of the internet and computer systems rather than specifically programming unlike AP Computer Science A. Students benefit from this course by being introduced to necessary concepts for a successful career and by being influenced to join the STEAM field. This course can pepare students for careers in front-end and back-end programming, IT, and careers in networking.

    Digital Media

    Students in this course learn to use technology to design and create art used in websites, apps, etc. This course expands the boundaries of how art is created and displayed; Digital Media is a perfect example of STEAM in the real world. Students benefit from this course by being influenced to go into the field of web designing, art, or front-end development.

    Technical Theater and Set Design

    This course allows students the oppurtunity to design and create elements of technical theater including, but not limited to, lighting, props, publicity, sound, and scenery for theatrical productions at their school, and within their community. The Technical Theater and Set Design class promotes the values of STEAM field by incluencing students to participate in the engineering, architectural, and construction side of theater and the arts. Some of the careers that this course prepares students for include: architect, engineer, construction manager, stage manager, and set designer/planner.

    Digital Video Production

    Digital Video Production teaches students the basics of using audio and visual devices and software in order to create videos and animations for their school, and community. Using video cameras provided by the school, students record short videos, movies, and shows and develop those videos for educational use. This course prepares students for careers related to filming, developing, editing, and distributing videos. Some of these careers include: video editor, film and TV producer, cameraman, screenwriter, and journalist.

About

The Osceola County School for the Arts was established in 2003 in the School District of Osceola County. OCSA is a performing arts school that selects the most talented musicians, dancers, actors, set engineers, and creative writers in the county.
